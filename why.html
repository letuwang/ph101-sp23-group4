Why is paper-waste such a big deal?!

We believe that reusing cardboard boxes is an essential part of reducing paper waste and mitigating the impact of climate change. Currently, packaging material and boxes are the highest consumed form of paper, and most people throw away or recycle perfectly usable boxes after receiving a package. This inefficient cycle adds up, resulting in significant amounts of paper waste and contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

Our solution to this problem is to establish cardboard box reusing centers in key areas, such as near the MLK's Amazon Locker Room area and in residential housing's mailing rooms. By incentivizing the return and recycling of packaging materials, we can encourage more people to participate in recycling and reusing efforts. This solution is more efficient and cost-effective than general recycling, which has high associated costs.

Our solution not only reduces paper waste but also helps to mitigate the impact of deforestation, which is a significant contributor to climate change. The primary production of paper involves cutting down trees, and reducing paper waste means that fewer trees need to be cut down, leading to less deforestation and a reduction in greenhouse gas emissions.

Our solution also offers a practical and accessible way for people to make a positive impact on the environment. By providing designated spots for cardboard boxes in trash rooms in residential halls and similar places, people who need boxes for shipping or moving can extend the use of those boxes, avoiding the need to buy new ones.

Overall, we believe that our solution of establishing cardboard box reusing centers and incentivizing the return and recycling of packaging materials is an effective and practical way to reduce paper waste and mitigate the impact of climate change. Join us in our mission to promote sustainable practices and make a positive impact on the environment.
